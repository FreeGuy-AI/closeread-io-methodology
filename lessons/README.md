# Lessons

The public subset of operating lessons learned while building the Closeread methodology and the surrounding business.

These lessons are sanitized for outside-the-team consumption. The internal lesson log lives in a private vault and contains operator IDs, substrate detail, and other context that does not belong on the public internet. The lessons that surface here are the ones whose pattern is durable enough to help a reader who is not part of the team.

The lessons fall into a few rough categories:

* **Structural defenses over behavioral checks.** Patterns where a behavioral safeguard turned out to be insufficient and the right fix was a structural defense (a linter, a pre-flight check, a tool-level enforcement). Most banked lessons live here.
* **Sub-agent orchestration discipline.** Patterns for delegating work to AI sub-agents reliably, including what to verify after a delegation and where the orchestrator's responsibility ends.
* **Outbound and identity discipline.** Patterns for managing the trust signals a small business sends to the world, including brand premise regression checks and identity file discipline.

## Current public entries

The first few public entries land within 30 days of launch, sanitized from the running internal lesson log. The internal log contains roughly twenty lessons at the time of writing; the public subset will be smaller.

If you want to see a specific lesson surfaced (because the pattern would help you), open an issue. We prioritize externally-useful banking over completeness.
